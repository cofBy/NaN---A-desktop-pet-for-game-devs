# What is a desktop pet?
A desktop pet is anything roaming around your screen while you do whatever you're doing. It's usually an animated creature that acts as a companion.

# Why use one?
I don't know they are always so annoying they don't do anything other than being useless. If you are not working they probably gonna be great.

But I wanted one that will help me code, So that's why I made my own desktop pet I named him "NaN"

# Why should I use NaN while coding?
His Apps that are above his head are the main feature of NaN

They are useful tools that are designed to save time for programmers

Here are every one of them and **A SINGLE** use case for every one:
- Pencil : Can be used for visualizing your thoughts
- Paper : It's used to save your quick links and folders to open quickly
- Calculator : Can be used for both simple math and complex stuff too
- Timer : Can be used for Pomodoro technique
- Sticky Notes : Can be used to remember what you were doing last time you worked on the project quickly.
- Ruler : Can measure the distance between two points in pixels (I don't know why would anyone want this)
- Color dropper : It's used for getting a certain color of a certain pixel on the screen

# How does it work?
It works by making the Windows API make the window's margins negative which basically inverts it giving an effect known as sheet of glass seen in [this tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RqgsGaMPZTw&t=77s) by the goat himself Code Monkey

Then, I used my handy tool [P.A.U.T](https://github.com/cofBy/P.A.U.T) to animate his legs and arms.

I also made a fullscreen shader that pixelates NaN and NaN, not only that but it also gives him an outline and changes his color to whatever you choose in the launcher

Did you notice that the app icons and every single UI sprite are pixelated renders of 3D models made in Blender? If you didn't it's probably because of the shading technique seen in [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBIPJdEECWg&t=641s) by KitagawaGameDev
