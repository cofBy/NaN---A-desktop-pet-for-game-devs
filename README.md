# What is a desktop pet?
A desktop pet is anything roaming around your screen while you do whatever your doing. It's usually an animated creature that acts as a companion

# Why use one?
I don't know they are always so anoying they don't do anything other than being usless. If you are not working they probably gonna be great.
But I wanted one that will help me code, So that's why I made my own desktop pet I named him "NaN"

# Why should I use NaN while coding?
His Apps that are above his head are the main pivot of NaN
They are useful tools that are desinged to save time for programmers

Here are every one of them and **A SINGLE** use case for every one:
- Pencil : Can be used for visulizing your thoughts
- Paper : It's used to save your quick links and folders to open quickly
- Calculator : Can be used for both simple math and complex stuff too
- Timer : Can be used for pomodoro techniuqe
- Sticky Notes : Can be used to remember what were you doing last time you worked on the project quickly.
- Ruler : Can measure the distance between two points in pixels (I don't know why would anyone want this)
- Color dropper : It's used for getting a certain color of a certain pixel on the screen

# How does it work?
It works by making the windoes api make the window's margins negative which basicaly inverts it giving an effect known as sheet of glass seen in [this tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RqgsGaMPZTw&t=77s) by the goat himself Code Money
Then, I used my handy tool [P.A.U.T](https://github.com/cofBy/P.A.U.T) to animate his legs and arms.
I also made a fullscreen shader that pixelates NaN and NaN, not only that but it also gives him an outline and changes his color to whatever you choosed in the lanucher
Did you notice that the apps icons and every single UI sprite Is a pixelated render of a 3d model made in blender, If you didn't it's probably because of the shading technique seen in [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PBIPJdEECWg&t=641s) by KitagawaGameDev
